AI-assisted Compton-camera reconstruction for ²²⁵Ac

End-to-end imaging pipeline for a liquid-xenon Compton camera built for targeted alpha therapy: GEANT4 simulation, GPU LM-OSEM reconstruction, kernel-density-estimated system matrices, and Bayesian optimisation of event-selection cuts.

Sponsor: TRIUMF (W. Fedorko, A. Sher) UBC ENPH capstone, 2025–26 Apr 2026

Download full report (PDF)↓

Summary

Targeted alpha therapy with ²²⁵Ac is a promising cancer treatment, but its clinical use depends on imaging where the radiopharmaceutical actually accumulates. Conventional SPECT loses most incoming photons to mechanical collimators; a Compton camera replaces that with electronic collimation, trading a harder reconstruction problem for much higher sensitivity.

I built a simulation-and-reconstruction framework for TRIUMF's TIIGR detector that images the 440 keV and 218 keV gamma lines of the ²²⁵Ac decay chain. The framework lets us characterise resolution, define automated quality metrics, and tune event-selection parameters with Bayesian optimisation.

// headline result
Bayesian optimisation of the scatter-energy acceptance window identified an optimal cut of [0, 122.6] keV, reducing the 30 mm two-source merge metric from m = 0.824 (uncut) to m = 0.529 at full convergence — a 35.8% improvement, while correctly identifying that the 30 mm separation remains intrinsically unresolvable at this source-detector distance.

Key technical ideas

Compton kinematics → cone reconstruction

Each detected event undergoes a Compton scatter in the inner detector and is then absorbed in the outer detector. From the deposited energies and interaction positions, the photon's origin is constrained to a cone whose opening angle follows the Compton kinematic relation:

$$ \cos\theta_C \;=\; 1 \;-\; \frac{m_e c^2 \, E_C}{E\,(E - E_C)} $$

where $E$ is the incident energy, $E_C$ the scatter deposit, and $m_e c^2$ the electron rest energy. Combining many such cones yields the 3-D source distribution.

LM-OSEM update on GPU

Reconstruction uses list-mode ordered-subsets EM. For voxel intensity $\lambda_j$ at iteration $k$, with subset $s$ of events:

$$ \lambda_j^{k+1} \;=\; \frac{\lambda_j^k}{s_j} \sum_{n \in s} \frac{A_j(y_n)}{\sum_l A_l(y_n)\,\lambda_l^k} $$

Per-event processing maps cleanly to GPU threads; I run 60 epochs on a uniform initial volume and checkpoint every epoch.

System matrix as a 2-D KDE

Cylindrical symmetry near the field of view collapses the system matrix to a position-invariant density $A(E_C, \theta) \approx P(E_C, \cos\theta)$. Rather than binning, I estimate it by 2-D kernel density estimation with an Epanechnikov kernel:

$$ \hat f(x) \;=\; \frac{1}{N h^d} \sum_{i=1}^{N} K\!\left(\frac{x - x_i}{h}\right) $$

The smooth density is more stable than direct binning when Monte Carlo statistics are limited, and gives the LM-OSEM forward model a well-behaved likelihood surface.

Two metrics that survive a parameter sweep

To make optimisation possible at all, I needed objectives that work programmatically. I defined:

  • a continuous merge metric $\;m = p_\text{valley} \,/\, \min\{p(x_L), p(x_R)\}\;$ on the x-axis marginal, with $m < 0.5$ as a Rayleigh-style resolution criterion;
  • a three-part activity-concentration quality metric that exploits known ground-truth source positions to flag diffuse, salt-and-pepper failures without manual image inspection.

Bayesian optimisation over event-selection cuts

Rather than hand-tuning the scatter-energy window, I treat $\;\text{merge metric}(E_\text{lo}, E_\text{hi})\;$ as a black-box function and optimise it with a Gaussian-process surrogate plus an acquisition function. The optimiser found [0, 122.6] keV — and, just as usefully, the shape of the surrogate showed when no cut could rescue resolution at all.

Methods used

simulation
GEANT4 Monte Carlo, ROOT output, configurable physics list, multi-seed batched runs
reconstruction
List-mode OSEM (LM-OSEM), 60 epochs, GPU-accelerated per-event update
system matrix
2-D kernel density estimation (Epanechnikov, scikit-learn) over $(E_C, \cos\theta)$
data interchange
ROOT → HDF5 staged pipeline with provenance metadata at every step
optimisation
Gaussian-process Bayesian optimisation of scatter-energy acceptance window
evaluation
Continuous merge metric (Rayleigh-style), activity-concentration quality metric, FWHM convergence
infrastructure
YAML configs, deterministic event selection, per-stage HDF5 checkpointing

What I'd do next

Two things take priority. First, a noise-free baseline study to isolate algorithmic from physical contributions to resolution. Second, a symmetry and degeneracy analysis of the system matrix — the salt-and-pepper failure I observed at sub-PSF voxel sizes is a rank-deficiency story, not a data-starvation one, and the next gain comes from regularisation rather than more events.
